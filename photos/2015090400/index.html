Cathédrale de Monaco


Cathédrale de Monaco, also known as Cathédrale Notre-Dame-Immaculée, or the Saint Nicholas Cathedral, is an important Roman Catholic church in Monte-Carlo, Monaco, where many of the Grimaldis were buried. The church was built in the end of 19th and beginning of the 20th century on a place of a previous, older cathedral that stood there until it was demolished in 1874. Taken on a rainy day, the white church stands out on the cloudy background, with wonderfully defined stone blocks.
